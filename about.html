Welcome

About Fortem Technologies, Inc.

Fortem Technologies, Inc. is a privately held, venture-backed company that delivers ultra-small SWaP-C radar for small manned aircraft as well as the data necessary for safe beyond line of sight (BLOS) unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) operations. The technology was developed and hardened over the last few years by a proven team focused on US Dept. of Defense applications and is available now to meet the security expectations of the public and the safety requirements of national regulatory agencies.

Why Radar?

Radio Detection and Ranging, or Radar, is a well understood and mature technology used in aircraft for detection of objects, largely independent of atmospheric obscurants such as clouds, dust, fog, rain, snow, and hail. Radar does not require moonlight or sunlight and is not adversely affected by lighting conditions.

Why Fortem Technologies?

Traditionally radar has been large, power hungry and expensive. Fortem Technologies brings to the market a radar from a proven team that is low cost, with a very small power, weight, and size footprint for use on UAS. With ranges from 1500m to 3000m+ and effective 360 degree coverage, the beginning of true autonomy beyond human line of site is now commercially viable.

Technology Heritage

Fortem Technologies, Inc. has purchased the UAS Detect and Avoid radar solution from IMSAR LLC. IMSAR LLC is the world leader in real time intelligence from small size, weight, and power high performance radar systems – seasoned with years of unmanned aircraft integration, primarily for DoD applications. No other company has solved the hard problems in a holistic manner that includes the complex mixture of RF, hardware, firmware, software, algorithms, radar, signal processing, and unmanned vehicles in a small, cost-effective package.

The radar systems have been operated with numerous manned and unmanned aircraft, both vertical takeoff and fixed wing platforms, in multiple countries throughout the world. They have flown on small and large platforms ranging from the hand launched Aerovironment Puma to the UH60 Helicopter. The radar is robust and meets rigorous MIL-STD-810-G qualifications for use in harsh environments.
