Introducing

Detect & Avoid Radar

DAA-R10

The DAA-R10 provides the aircraft integrator with a radar to quickly and easily detect and avoid. Simplicity of integration is a serial connection, 18-36V input voltage, and a graphical web-page configuration over ethernet. Mounting points allow flexibility to meet the needs of specific aircraft integrations. The DAA-R10 consists of a fully functional, bits-in and bits-out radar unit, communication and power cable, Quick Start Guide, and Interface Control Document (ICD).

DAA-R20

The DAA-R20 is functionally equivalent to the DAA-R10. It is designed for high volume, high reliability, mission critical detect and avoid operation. The DAA-R20 design conforms to FAA and industry design and safety standards.

Product Specifications

Scanning

•  Built-in proprietary antenna technology.
•  Azimuth field of view (FOV) is 125˚.
•  Elevation FOV is 40˚.
•  Simultaneous continuous tracking of multiple threats.
•  Proven algorithms for high accuracy.

Intruder and Threat Information

•  Detects 3m² RCS (Radar Cross Section) from 1500m.
•  Detection of construction cranes, towers, gliders, balloonists, and terrain.
•  Outputs are range/distance, 2˚, velocity (range rate), threat size (radar cross section).
•  A ‘track’ will show the threat velocity vector.

Interfaces

•  The sensor is a compact sensor with bits in and bits out.
•  Serial or Ethernet connectors for control and data are alongside a power connector.
•  Serial RS-232 for outputs of threat detections.
•  Serial RS-232 for inputs from GPS or altitude, heading reference data.
•  Ethernet web-interface for easy configuration.
•  18-36V DC.

Data

•  Intruder detection is performed real time on board.
•  Data from the aircraft on location, heading, and attitude is consumed by the radar to distinguish between target movement and aircraft motion.
•  Data Rate: Intruder track data rate is under 3kHz per intruder with a 10 Hz update rate.
•  Data outputs for tracks are a track ID, 3D position and velocity vector, and threat magnitude. An optional threat level indicator field distinguishes non-threat targets from potential collision threats.
•  All measurements from the sensor come with associated measurement uncertainties.

Standard Certification

The sensors are designed to UL, CE, CSA, DO-160, DO-178, and DO-254 standards.

Built In Test

The sensor comes with a reference channel for built in calibration (BIC), and continuous built in test (CBIT). This unique capability serves as a means of self-reporting failures.

Export Considerations: ITAR vs EAR

The sensor falls under EAR on the Commerce Control List (CCL).

About

Fortem Technologies is a privately held, venture-backed company that delivers ultra small SWaP-C radar to enable, for the first time, completely autonomous beyond line of sight unmanned aircraft vehicle operations. The radar is also used on manned aircraft to provide the human pilot with assisted detect and avoid capability. Fully autonomous beyond line of sight operations are now possible due to this technology - now available for the first time - to commercial markets worldwide.
